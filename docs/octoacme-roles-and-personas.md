# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee end-to-end release planning and execution, managing the release schedule and coordinating across development, QA, and operations teams. They ensure clear communication and smooth handoffs from development to production.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules with cross-functional teams
- Manage release notes, documentation, and communications
- Coordinate with Developers and QA to ensure readiness for deployment
- Track release dependencies and critical path items
- Manage rollback procedures and post-deployment verification
- Liaise with operations teams on deployment windows and infrastructure needs

### Goals
- Ensure predictable, low-risk releases to production
- Reduce time between development completion and user availability
- Maintain clear visibility and communication across release phases

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with PM, engineering, and ops
- Release notes and stakeholder announcements
- Deployment coordination in project board and release checklists
- Post-release retrospectives and lessons learned

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Review code readiness and technical dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Validate acceptance and sign-off on release quality
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate timeline and dependency management
- **Operations**: Manage infrastructure and deployment execution

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers define user experience flows, design interfaces, and advocate for usability. They conduct research and testing to ensure features meet user needs and are intuitive to use.

### Responsibilities
- Define user flows and information architecture
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Collaborate on design review and iteration cycles
- Ensure accessibility standards and best practices are met
- Provide design feedback during development and QA phases

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support burden through good design
- Ensure consistency across product features

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with Developers and Product Managers
- User research findings and usability test results
- Design documentation and component specifications

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Validate designs against user needs and business goals
- **Developers**: Collaborate on implementation and technical feasibility
- **QA/Testing**: Test and validate usability and accessibility compliance

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business stakeholders and technical teams by gathering requirements, mapping business processes, and translating needs into actionable specifications. They ensure that solutions address real business problems.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Map current and future business processes
- Translate business needs into technical specifications and acceptance criteria
- Identify process improvements and optimization opportunities
- Validate solutions against business objectives
- Document decisions and rationale for future reference

### Goals
- Ensure alignment between business needs and technical solutions
- Reduce rework due to unclear or misaligned requirements
- Improve process efficiency through thoughtful analysis

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions and stakeholder interviews
- Process flow diagrams and specification documents
- Backlog refinement and acceptance criteria definition

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Collaborate on prioritization and business impact analysis
- **Project Manager**: Clarify scope and identify dependencies
- **Developers**: Ensure specifications are clear and implementable

---

## Customer Support Lead

### Role Summary
Customer Support Leads represent the voice of the customer to the project team, bringing real-world feedback and insights. They prepare the support organization for new features and ensure a smooth customer experience at launch.

### Responsibilities
- Gather customer feedback and pain points from support interactions
- Prepare support documentation, FAQs, and troubleshooting guides
- Partner with Product and Development teams on feature readiness
- Ensure support team readiness for launches and feature changes
- Monitor early adoption issues and escalate critical customer problems
- Provide insights on usability and feature adoption challenges

### Goals
- Reduce support volume through good documentation and design
- Ensure positive customer experience at feature launch
- Provide early warning signals on product issues or confusion

### Typical Communication
- Feature readiness reviews and support training sessions
- Support FAQs and knowledge base contributions
- Customer feedback summaries and trend analysis

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Provide customer feedback for iteration and prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate feature launch and communication timelines
- **QA/Testing**: Validate user-facing behavior and edge cases

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
Security Engineers embed security practices into project execution, reviewing features for vulnerabilities and ensuring that solutions meet security and compliance standards. They partner with development and operations teams to maintain a secure product.

### Responsibilities
- Review features and architectural designs for security risks
- Conduct security testing and vulnerability assessments
- Define security requirements and compliance standards
- Partner with Developers on secure implementation practices
- Prepare security documentation and incident response procedures
- Validate deployment and operational security controls

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities and data breaches
- Maintain compliance with security and regulatory standards
- Embed security practices into the development lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Security design reviews and threat modeling sessions
- Security test results and remediation tracking
- Release security sign-offs and incident response protocols

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Review code and architecture for security best practices
- **Release Manager**: Validate security posture before production deployment
- **Project Manager**: Escalate critical security risks and blockers
